Lab Resources

Nanomagnetism and Thin Films Lab

The Nanomagnetism and Thin Films Lab is a specialized facility dedicated to studying the properties and applications of nanoscale magnetic materials and thin films. It is equipped with state-of-the-art instruments and techniques to investigate the behavior of magnetic materials at the nanoscale level.

Researchers in this lab utilize various experimental setups to synthesize, characterize, and manipulate nanomagnetic materials. They employ techniques such as molecular beam epitaxy, sputtering, and chemical vapor deposition to fabricate thin films with precise control over their thickness and composition.

Alternative Energy and Nanotechnology Lab

The Alternative Energy and Nanotechnology Lab is a research facility dedicated to exploring innovative solutions for sustainable energy production and utilization using nanotechnology. This lab focuses on developing and optimizing nanomaterials and devices for various alternative energy technologies, such as solar cells, fuel cells, and energy storage systems.

Researchers in this lab employ nanoscale engineering techniques to enhance the efficiency, durability, and cost-effectiveness of energy conversion and storage devices. They work on synthesizing and characterizing nanomaterials like quantum dots, nanowires, and nanocomposites, which can exhibit unique properties such as enhanced light absorption, catalytic activity, or charge transport.

Glass and Ceramic Lab

The Glass and Ceramic Lab is a specialized facility dedicated to the synthesis, characterization, and testing of glass and ceramic materials. This lab is equipped with the necessary tools and equipment to study the properties and behavior of these materials under different conditions.

Researchers in this lab work on the formulation and synthesis of glass and ceramic compositions, utilizing techniques such as melting, annealing, and crystallization. They investigate the effects of various additives and processing parameters on the resulting material properties, such as transparency, hardness, thermal expansion, and chemical resistance.

Glass Cutting and Polishing Unit

A facility equipped with machinery for precision cutting and polishing of glass components. It enables accurate shaping and finishing of glass materials for various applications in industries such as optics and electronics.

Glass Melting Furnace

A specialized furnace used for melting glass raw materials at high temperatures. It allows for the creation of molten glass that can be further processed into different glass products.

High Temperature Furnace

A furnace designed to reach and maintain extremely high temperatures for materials processing. It is used for heat treatment, sintering, and other applications that require controlled high-temperature environments.

Hydraulic Press

A machine that utilizes hydraulic force to shape and compress materials. It is commonly used in industrial settings for tasks such as metalworking and material testing.

Ultrasonicator

A device that employs high-frequency sound waves to clean objects through the process of cavitation. It is often used in laboratories and industrial settings for delicate or intricate items.

Hot Air Oven

A thermal device used for controlled heating and drying of materials. It finds applications in various fields, including laboratories, pharmaceuticals, and food processing, for tasks such as drying samples or removing moisture from objects.
